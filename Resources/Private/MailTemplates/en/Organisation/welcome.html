How to get started

Your organization will be granted an administrative user account, the organization manager. (see above). This user account has privileges to invite other users to your organization, or revoke their membership.
Your backend account grants access to verification management and the Visual Skill Editor.

To invite your colleagues to your organization, you can create invite codes and distribute them as you see fit. Created user profiles are private by default, with no profile data visible to anyone else outside your company.

Find out more about managing your organization on SkillDisplay in our ServiceDesk.

Manage your Organization
 

Appoint Verifiers

Verification rights can be distributed among your organization’s members, according to expertise or however you see fit. Access the SkillDisplay Backend and switch to the Verifier Permissions tab to select the SkillSets and verification types you want to assign.

Assigning Verification Permissions to Organization Members
 

Create your own Skills

Before you start to create your own skills, we suggest you get used to the SkillDisplay Methodology, and learn how skills are defined, structured, and how the different types of verification work.

Basic concepts of the SkillDisplay skill management methodology
 

When you feel ready to start skill creation, we suggest starting with a single, well focused SkillSet with only a small number of skills. That way you cannot get lost in defining dependencies and formulating skill data. After your first steps you can build on your created skills, create new ones, or break existing skills down into more detailed skills.
